\title{FeBEx: In-Network Deduplication for LoRaWAN Backhaul Using P4 Programmable Switches}

\begin{abstract}
LoRaWAN and Helium networks solve the last-mile connectivity problem for IoT devices, but their fundamental architecture creates a critical inefficiency: when a device broadcasts, multiple geographically distributed gateways independently forward the same packet through backhaul to cloud backends. This redundancy, essential for reliability, multiplies backhaul traffic and cost.

We present FeBEx, a P4-programmable aggregation switch that moves deduplication from cloud backends to the network edge. FeBEx implements register-based deduplication with epoch-driven expiry, integrated with Longest-Prefix-Match tenant steering and packet cloning for Proof-of-Coverage attribution. Through comprehensive evaluation on Mininet-BMv2, we demonstrate that FeBEx achieves 50-90\% backhaul bandwidth savings while maintaining perfect delivery ratio (no false suppressions), 100\% multi-tenant isolation, and accurate payment attribution. We provide practical guidelines: register size $\geq 4N$, epoch intervals of 5-10 seconds, and validate effectiveness across city-scale deployments (50-500 devices, 5-50 gateways). This work demonstrates that edge-based deduplication is viable, effective, and ready for deployment on production P4 switches.
\end{abstract}
